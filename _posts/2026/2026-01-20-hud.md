## Holographic Optical Element (HOE)
<p></p>

* Physics of HOE : [How are holograms possible?](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EmKQsSDlaa4). [HOE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holographic_optical_element) produces holographic images using principles of diffraction.
* [HOLOGRAPHIC OPTICAL ELEMENTS: Printing technology enables HOE volume manufacturing](https://www.laserfocusworld.com/optics/article/16563268/holographic-optical-elements-printing-technology-enables-hoe-volume-manufacturing).
* [3D head-up display with a multiple extended depth of field based on integral imaging and holographic optical elements](https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.14315?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

A HOE is recorded once: interference of reference beam + object beam.
This encodes a fixed wavefront transformation.
When you illuminate it with the reference beam (or its conjugate), it outputs exactly that predefined wavefront.
✅ Works great for fixed images, symbols, HUD marks.
❌ Cannot directly show arbitrary dynamic content


**Modern HUDs use HOE** for dynamic images.
The trick: decouple the “optical transformation” from the “image content”.
Think of HOE as an optical **“light guide / lens / mirror”**, not the display itself.

* Holographic Display: [Ceres Holographics CES 2025](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9hEq5owqmLk) & [MOBIS Tech: Holographic Windshield Display](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tItXoT-VAQ0).
* HUD solution : [Varjo VR Holographic Vehicle HUD](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dCza8rNXDVM) with eye tracking, augmented reality.

## Autostereoscopy
<p></p>

* **Parallax Barrier Displays** : A parallax barrier is a layer with precisely spaced slits placed in front of a conventional display. These slits block specific pixels from each eye, directing different pixel columns to the left and right eyes.
